Listens like Mel: Boosting Latent Audio Diffusion with Channel Locality

Channel-span masking induces a mel-like spectral bias in high-compression VAE latents, acting as a low-pass filter across channels. Leveraging this effect, we present Latent Flow MAE (LAFA), a 55M-parameter lightweight VAE bottleneck that strengthens local channel correlations and unveils structured locality in the latent space.

Overview

Latent representations critically shape diffusion-based audio generation. We observe that Mel spectrograms exhibit an approximate power-law spectrum that aligns with diffusion’s coarse-to-fine denoising, whereas waveform variational autoencoder latents are nearly equal intensity along the channel axis. We introduce channel-span masking, which in expectation behaves like a rectangular window across channels and thus a low-pass filter in the channel-frequency domain, increasing channel locality. The induced locality steepens latent spectral slopes toward a power-law distribution and leads to up to 4× faster convergence of Diffusion Transformer training on audio generation tasks, while maintaining reconstruction fidelity and compression. Experimental results show that the model performs comparably to, or better than, competitive baselines under the same conditions.

Sound Generation

We evaluate sound generation on the AudioCaps dataset. AudioLDM2 and TANGO2 are MelVAE-based diffusion models that generate mono 16 kHz audio, whereas SAO (Stable Audio Open) employs a waveform VAE to synthesize stereo 44.1 kHz high-fidelity audio. Our method adopts the DiT architecture of SAO and leverages its pretrained waveform VAE as the foundation, while introducing LAFA to refine the latent representation quality.

Results.

Our approach surpasses SAO in semantic plausibility and event coherence, producing audio that more accurately aligns with the textual prompts. At the same time, it retains the high-fidelity characteristics of waveform VAEs.

Results.

Ablation Study

We assess the effectiveness of LAFA through an ablation study on music generation, employing models trained for 250k iterations. The proposed method yields cleaner and more coherent musical structures during early training and achieves better temporal alignment with the conditioning duration.

Reconstrcution Results

We evaluate reconstruction on both audio and music tasks. AudioLDM2 employs a MelVAE with a HiFi-GAN vocoder to reconstruct mono 16 kHz waveforms, whereas SAO directly decodes waveform latents into stereo 44.1 kHz audio with 13× higher compression. LAFA preserves the high fidelity of waveform VAE while enhancing latent-space organization and reconstruction robustness through rectified-flow refinement.
